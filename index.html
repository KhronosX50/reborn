Community · 4 min read

Format

Rules of Engagement

The official Reborn! debate format. Read before you step into the arena.

I. Debate Structure

1

Debater A — Win Condition

Debater A opens by stating their Win Condition — the specific argument, feat, or ability they believe secures victory for their character or position. This must be grounded in canon evidence.

2

Debater B — Resistance + Counter (Win Condition)

Debater B responds with two things in one move: a Resistance that neutralises A's Win Condition, and a Counter — which simultaneously serves as B's own Win Condition. Both must be supported with evidence.

3

Debater A — Resistance

Debater A responds to B's Counter/Win Condition with their own Resistance. This is A's final move. A must demonstrate why B's Win Condition does not hold against their character or argument.

⚖

Judges — Verdict

Judges evaluate both Win Conditions and all evidence presented. The verdict is based on the strength, consistency, and canon validity of each debater's arguments. The judge's decision is final.

II. Time Limit

Each debater has 24 hours to post their argument from the moment it is their turn. Failure to respond within 24 hours results in an automatic forfeit of that round. Extensions may be granted at the admin's discretion under exceptional circumstances.

III. Evidence & Validity Rules

✓

Canon sources only. All evidence must come from primary canon — manga, anime, light novels, databooks, or official statements from authors/creators. Fan wikis are not primary sources and cannot be cited as standalone evidence.

✓

No outlier feats. A feat is considered an outlier if it is a significant, unexplained anomaly that contradicts the character's consistent performance throughout the series. Outlier feats may not be used as a primary basis for a Win Condition.

✓

No wanking or lowballing. Arguments must represent a character's honest, consistent capabilities. Deliberately overstating or understating a character's abilities to win an argument is grounds for disqualification by the judges.

✓

Scaling must be logical and traceable. If you scale a character through another, each step of the scaling chain must be supported by explicit or strongly implied canon evidence. Vague or speculative scaling chains are invalid.

✓

Statements vs. feats. Demonstrated feats take priority over character statements. Author statements are admissible but may be challenged if they contradict in-universe evidence.

IV. Conduct

✗

Personal attacks, insults, or bad faith arguments are not tolerated. Debate the argument, not the person.

✗

Deliberate misrepresentation of an opponent's argument is prohibited. Engage with what was actually said.

✗

Judges' verdicts are final. Prolonged disputing of a verdict in the debate thread is considered unsportsmanlike conduct.
